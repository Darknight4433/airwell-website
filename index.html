The Plants Provided by Us

Coleus (Plectranthus scutellarioides)

Effectiveness: Highly responsive to ozone and other pollutants, with visible leaf discoloration.

Feature: Colorful foliage that changes under stress, making pollution detection easy to observe.

Geranium

Effectiveness: Sensitive to sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides, showing spots or yellowing leaves.

Feature: Easy to grow and maintain, with clear visual signs of air pollution stress.

Peace Lily (Spathiphyllum)

Peace lilies can absorb toxins like benzene and formaldehyde. They might show yellowing of leaves or reduced growth under polluted conditions.

Boston Ferns

Boston Ferns are good at filtering out formaldehyde and other indoor air pollutants. They may show browning or drying of fronds in highly polluted environments.
